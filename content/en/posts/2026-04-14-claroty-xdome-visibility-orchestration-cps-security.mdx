## Executive Summary
Claroty has announced new Visibility Orchestration features for its xDome SaaS platform, designed to transform the concept of cyber-physical system (CPS) visibility into a quantifiable and actionable security metric. The capability is positioned as a method for organizations to measure and prove the effectiveness of their CPS security programs, moving beyond qualitative assessments.

## Technical Analysis
The Visibility Orchestration feature functions as an analytical layer within the Claroty xDome platform. According to the vendor's announcement, it processes data from an organization's deployed security sensors and other data sources to calculate a proprietary "Visibility Score." This score is intended to serve as a benchmark for the completeness of an organization's asset inventory and security coverage across its operational technology (OT), Internet of Things (IoT), and other CPS environments. The technical specifics of the scoring algorithm, including its exact inputs, weighting, and validation methodology, are not publicly disclosed in the source material. The feature's output is designed to guide security teams in prioritizing remediation and sensor deployment efforts to areas with the lowest measured visibility.

## Threat Actor Context
This announcement does not pertain to a specific threat actor or campaign. It addresses a foundational security challenge—incomplete asset visibility—that is exploited by a wide range of threat actors targeting critical infrastructure and enterprise environments. A lack of comprehensive visibility into CPS assets creates blind spots that adversaries can leverage for initial access, lateral movement, and impact.

## Mitigations & Recommendations
The primary recommendation from the source is the adoption of tools capable of providing measurable visibility into CPS assets. Organizations are advised to move beyond simple asset discovery and implement continuous monitoring solutions that can classify assets, identify vulnerabilities, and detect anomalies. Specifically, for users of the Claroty xDome platform, leveraging the new Visibility Orchestration feature to establish a baseline visibility score and then systematically working to improve that score through targeted sensor deployment and asset management is suggested. More broadly, security programs protecting OT, IoT, and IoMT (Internet of Medical Things) should prioritize achieving and maintaining a high-fidelity, continuously updated asset inventory as a cornerstone of their defense strategy.
